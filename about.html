Our Inception

Born from a Promise, Built on Hope

In the winter of 2012, our co-founder Dr. Anita Sharma watched a 4-year-old girl named Meena pass away waiting for a heart surgery her family could not afford. The surgery would have cost ₹1.8 lakhs—a sum raised on social media in just 3 days, but Meena passed away two days before the surgery could happen.

Dr. Sharma promised to build a transparent, localized network of care so no child would ever lose their life to poverty. Stackly Global was born.

We operate across 22 states in India and 18 developing countries with a network of partner clinics, schools, and nutrition kitchens. We map every donation to physical aid delivery, tracking every student, patient, and daily meal logs.

Vetted Integrity

Our Journey

Key Milestones

From a single clinic to a global network of hope.

2012 — Foundation
Stackly Global was born with a single mobile clinic in Salem, Tamil Nadu, serving 500+ families in the first year.
2015 — Digital Schools
Launched our first digital classrooms, bringing tablet-based learning to 5,000+ students in 30+ rural schools.
2018 — Water Initiative
Installed our first 20 solar-powered water filtration micro-plants, providing clean water to 10,000+ people daily.
2022 — Global Expansion
Expanded operations to 18+ developing countries, reaching 120,000+ children in total.
2026 — 1M Meals
Achieved our 1 million meals served milestone, with ZeroHunger kitchens serving nutritious meals daily to 50,000+ children.
